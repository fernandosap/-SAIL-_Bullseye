Oliver Ramaiah - Bullseye CTO


Bullseye announced a series of leadership changes today, including tapping 14-year industry veteran Oliver Ramaiah as its new chief transformation officer. 


Oliver, who also assumes the title of executive vice president, has held a number of IT and transformation roles in the industry since 2009, most recently serving as senior vice president of digital business transformation for Hacks 4th avenue.


As CTO, Oliver will be responsible for supporting strategic objectives of Growth and Market Share, Shareholders and Market Capitalization, Sustainability, and strategic longevity, while supporting the following Lines of business with the following business objectives:


    • Head of Customer Experience – Conversion rate and Sales Growth
    • Head of Merchandising – New Revenue, Process efficiency 
    • Head of Procurement – Margin, Quality, and Supplier Performance
    • Head of Supply Chain – Inventory Accuracy, on-time deliveries, costs, and wastage
    • Head of store operations – Experiential Retail

“The changes we’re announcing create new opportunities for key leaders to bring their knowledge and experiences to different areas of the business,” said Bullseye CEO Jessica Zhang in a statement. “I’m confident these moves will drive Bullseye’s position as a best-in-class retailer today and well into the future.”
